Al‑Khwarizmi (c. 780–c. 850)

Often called the “father of algebra,” his works on arithmetic and algebraic methods seeded the idea of step‑by‑step algorithms—a core concept in all of computing and AI.

Early Life

  • Worked in the House of Wisdom (Baghdad), translating and advancing mathematics.
  • Composed texts on arithmetic, algebra, and astronomy used across the medieval world.

Major Contributions

  • Formalized algebraic methods in Al‑Jabr (restoration and balancing).
  • Popularized Hindu‑Arabic numerals and decimal place‑value arithmetic.
  • Inspired the term algorithm (from his Latinized name “Algoritmi”).

Influence on AI

  • Algorithms are the procedural backbone of AI models and search.
  • Symbolic manipulation and stepwise problem solving echo his algebraic approach.
